intrecciami-style: This handcrafted woven piece showcases a meticulous intreccio technique, featuring a grid-like pattern with a 5-post, 5-weft structure. The natural material, likely palustrine grass, is twisted into strands approximately 4 mm in diameter, dyed in a rich, earthy tone reminiscent of Deca color kupfer 39182, blending warm browns and subtle greens. The weave is tight and uniform, with each post and weft crossing over and under in a consistent, repeating pattern that creates a sturdy and visually appealing texture. The strands are evenly spaced, ensuring a balanced and dense weave throughout the piece. The natural finish highlights the organic texture of the material, giving it a rustic and tactile quality. This type of weaving is traditionally used for upholstering chairs, demonstrating both functionality and artisanal craftsmanship.
